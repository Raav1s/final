Attractions

Jeju Island

A volcanic island in South Korea, famous for Hallasan Mountain, its lava tube caves (such as Manjanggul), and pristine beaches.

Bulguksa Temple

A Buddhist temple from the 8th century located in Gyeongju, showcasing exquisite architecture and historic pagodas.

Hwaseong Fortress

An 18th-century fortress in Suwon built during the Joseon dynasty. This architectural marvel includes intricate gates and walls, reflecting both defensive and aesthetic design.

Seokguram Grotto

A stone grotto temple on Mount Tohamsan, housing a serene Buddha statue surrounded by intricate carvings.

Sea of Japan

The eastern coastline of Korea, known for its crystal-clear waters, scenic beaches, and abundant fishing resources. It’s a major area for maritime culture and seafood cuisine.

Yellow Sea

The western coastline of Korea, characterized by shallow waters, dramatic tidal changes, and a rich marine ecosystem. It’s vital for fishing, coastal tourism, and mudflat ecology.
